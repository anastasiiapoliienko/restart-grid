D · Where does the 9% live?

800 scenarios × 3 trained policies, binned by outage duration (rows) and ambient temperature (columns). Each cell shows the PPO trip rate for scenarios falling in that bin. The honest finding: failures are dispersed across the parameter space rather than concentrated in one corner — there's no single "easy fix," which is why Phase 1's safety shield is the right next attack rather than just more training data.

What this says

No clean failure corner. The worst column is +1 to +3 °C — slightly counter-intuitive (the policy actually handles deep cold better than mild ambient, because deep cold produces more uniformly heavy CLPU it has already seen, while mild ambient gives variable inrush the policy guesses wrong on).

The path to 0% trip isn't "train more on the worst column." It's a safety shield: a forecasting head that vetoes any closure expected to cross 240 A. PPO chooses, the shield checks.
